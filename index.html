About Me

I am a maker! I love building stuff (especially dumb sh*t). I've been involved with more interesting projects I can list here.

In 2012, I've built the first educational augmented reality app in Brazil, way before AR was cool. In 2014, I've built the first VR experience to be showcased at the Sao Paulo International book biennial, months before Oculus acquisition by Facebook was announced.

My career is non-linear even in higher dimensional spaces. I’ve been an intern, developer, tech lead and CTO at one company, then freelancer and back to intern; researcher and back to intern; senior software engineer and back to intern. All that while doing my undergrad.
I am, by definition, a jack-of-all-trades, but now that I am finishing my Masters, I will be master of one. Also did I mention I am an intern again?

I lived in two different countries, 10 different cities, started two degrees, finished one, speak 3 different languages, programmed professionally in 6, maybe by the time you read this I will be in a full-time position, or maybe at some point, I will start my PhD and likely will have to go back to being an intern. Who knows?
All I know all those changes allowed me to work at some very exciting projects during my life.

Passion and willingness to learn made my trajectory quite unusual and that’s who I am: an unusual person.

But I digress, those are some of the awesome (and some not so awesome) stuff I’ve done over the years.

Deep Learning for Breast Cancer Detection

Breast cancer is the most commonly occurring cancer in women and the second most common cancer overall. Being able to detect it early and efficiently can have a huge impact reducing the need for invasive techniques. For this project, I used a generative adversarial network (GAN) built in Python to detect malignant cancer cells in histopathology images.

MyCity.ID

MyCity.ID is a self-sovereign digital ID designed to improve the verification of identity-related information. It speeds up the process of applying for social services and benefits by using pre-verified information, making the process easier for residents and service providers. MyCity.ID uses a private blockchain network that keeps individuals' information secure and allows them to control who can see and use their ID.

Kate - Machine Intelligence Project

During my internship at IBM I collaborated with a team of three scientists and five interns to develop a software using C++ that controlled the walking mechanism of a bipedal robot. For the project, we utilized a signal processing algorithm to handle information from the robot’s sensors and camera and create a machine learning model to predict an impending fall. Created a software that would enable the robot to stop and regain balance upon an impending fall, increasing the robot’s steps, on average, by 500.
